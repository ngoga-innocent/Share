Our Story

Small in scale yet impactful in its reach, SHARE's journey is a testament to our unwavering commitment to solidarity, service, and the Common Good. Rooted in purpose and driven by a deep sense of responsibility, we strive to make a meaningful difference, fostering a spirit of collaboration and support that transcends boundaries.

2005: The Beginning

SHARE was officially founded as "Share Urumuri Ass Tubasange," although its voluntary efforts began even earlier. The journey commenced with a focus on providing financial and educational support to orphans and children from underprivileged families in Rwanda, helping them access quality education and healthcare. In addition to these efforts, SHARE emphasized teaching the values of sharing, fostering a spirit of solidarity and mutual support among the younger generation.

2010: In Response to the Impact of the Genocide

In the aftermath of the genocide, SHARE has focused its efforts in Rwanda on education, healthcare, cooperative workgroups, and youth empowerment. Guided by the principle of Ndi Umunyarwanda, which promotes unity and solidarity, SHARE works to foster healing, resilience, and collective responsibility, helping communities recover and rebuild from the past.

The first Shiatsu and natural healing training center was established in Rwanda for deaf children, empowering them to unlock their potential and become self-reliant.

Present: Centers for the Culture of the Common Good

SHARE envisions the Centers for the Culture of the Common Good as exemplary models of solidarity and the cultivation of human values. These centers are designed to foster lasting positive impact, promoting harmony between individuals, communities, and the environment. Through these initiatives, we strive to nurture a culture of mutual respect, sustainable development, and shared prosperity for all.
