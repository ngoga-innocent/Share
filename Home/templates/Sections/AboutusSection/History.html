Our Story

Small and intense, SHARE's journey reflects a profound commitment to solidarity, service, and the Common Good.

2001: The Beginning

SHARE was officially established as the "Oki do Human Life Project," though its voluntary activities started earlier. The journey began with building wells for nomadic populations in Morocco.

1999: Kosovo War Relief

SHARE supported Kosovo refugees in Albania and Macedonia through four missions, providing humanitarian aid and offering hope during a devastating war.

2000: Cambodia & Myanmar

SHARE launched initiatives in Cambodia and Myanmar, implementing sustainable projects like wells, schools, study support, and training for rural communities.

2004: Shiatsu Healing in Myanmar

The first Shiatsu and natural healing training center was established in Myanmar for deaf children, empowering them to unlock their potential and become self-reliant.

2005: A New Chapter in Rwanda

SHARE began efforts in Rwanda post-genocide, focusing on education, healthcare, cooperative work groups, and creating spaces for youth empowerment.

Present: Centers for the Culture of the Common Good

SHARE envisions Centers for the Culture of the Common Good as models for solidarity and the development of human values, creating a lasting impact in harmony with the environment and society.
